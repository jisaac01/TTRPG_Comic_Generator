Target panel count: {panel_count}

For each panel, output:
- beat_indices
- beat_summary
- story_purpose
- panel_scale
- panel_shape
- setting_brief
- character_focus
- must_include
- dialogue_goals
- continuity_notes

Rules:
- Keep panels in strict chronological order.
- Cover the major beats; each beat should appear in at least one panel.
- Use panel_scale and panel_shape to express pacing and emphasis. The climax gets the largest panel.
- beat_summary must be a one-sentence summary of the selected beat_indices for that panel.
- Keep setting_brief concise and structural, not fully scripted.
- dialogue_goals must express what a character would say or the emotional register of the line — not a description of their action. If a memorable spoken line exists in the story text, quote it directly in dialogue_goals.
- Use must_include for visual facts the scriptwriter must preserve.
- Keep must_include panel-local: include only details that are central to this panel's assigned beat(s).
- Keep must_include concise and specific (target 1-3 concrete items).
- Preserve distinctive recap details (named artifacts, unique creatures, named locations, and specific discoveries) in must_include.
- Use continuity_notes for state that should persist across adjacent panels.
- The final panel must end on the most compelling hook available: wonder, dread, discovery, or unresolved tension.
- Fully consume the story text for downstream writing: capture all plot-critical details in the panel fields above because the scriptwriter will not receive raw story text.
- Do not leave essential story information only in your private reasoning; encode it explicitly in beat_summary, must_include, dialogue_goals, and continuity_notes.
