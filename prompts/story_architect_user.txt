For each panel, output:

Rules:
- Keep panels in strict chronological order.
- Cover the major beats; each beat should appear in at least one panel.
- Use panel_scale and panel_shape to express pacing and emphasis. The climax gets the largest panel.
- beat_summary must be a one-sentence summary of the selected beat_indices for that panel.
- Keep setting_brief concise and structural, not fully scripted.
- If a memorable spoken line exists in Reference quotes and it is actually meaningful, place it in notable_quotes for the panel where it is spoken.
- notable_quotes must be a list of objects... Only use lines from the provided Reference quotes list. Assign to the precise chronological panel. Never duplicate across panels.
- Each notable_quotes entry must be an object with keys: text, speaker, attribution_context.
- notable_quotes.text must exactly match a line from Reference quotes (no paraphrases).
- notable_quotes.speaker must name who says the line.
- notable_quotes.attribution_context must describe what the line refers to in this scene.
- Do not place non-spoken labels (for example, inscription text like "Payment") in notable_quotes unless that exact line appears in Reference quotes as spoken dialogue.
- Omit weak, throwaway, or contextless lines from notable_quotes.
- dialogue_goals must describe the intended voice, subtext, or emotional register of the panel's dialogue, not a description of action and not a duplicate of notable_quotes.
- Use notable_set_dressing for visually important scene objects and environmental details the scriptwriter should preserve.
- Keep notable_set_dressing panel-local: include only details that are central to this panel's assigned beat(s).
- Keep notable_set_dressing concise and specific (target 1-3 concrete items).
- Preserve distinctive recap details (named artifacts, unique creatures, named locations, and specific discoveries) in notable_set_dressing.
- Use continuity_notes for state that should persist across adjacent panels.
- The final panel must end on the most compelling hook available: wonder, dread, discovery, or unresolved tension.
- Fully consume the story text for downstream writing: capture all plot-critical details in the panel fields above because the scriptwriter will not receive raw story text.
- Do not leave essential story information only in your private reasoning; encode it explicitly in beat_summary, notable_set_dressing, notable_quotes, dialogue_goals, and continuity_notes.
