Structural & Panel Terms
Grid: The underlying layout of panels on a page (e.g., a "nine-panel grid").

Tier: A single horizontal row of panels. A script might call for a "three-tier page."

Splash Page: A single image that takes up the entire page, used for impact or establishing a new setting.

Double-Page Spread (DPS): A single giant image spanning two facing pages.

In-set Panel: A small panel placed entirely within the borders of a larger panel. This is often used to show a detail (like a character’s eyes) without breaking the flow of the main action.

Bleed: When the art "bleeds" off the edge of the physical page rather than being contained by a white border.

Narrative Overlays & Text Direction
Caption: The term for narrative text that is not dialogue. These are usually contained in rectangular boxes.

Voice Over (V.O.): Used in captions to indicate a character is narrating the scene from a different time or place.

Chyron: Borrowed from broadcast, this refers to text overlays that indicate location or time (e.g., "London, 1821").

Burst: A jagged or star-shaped bubble used for shouting or loud sound effects (SFX).

Transitions & Pacing
Panel-to-Panel: The logic of what happens in the "gutter" (the space between panels).

Aspect-to-Aspect: A transition that stays within a single scene but moves the "camera" around to different elements to set a mood.

Moment-to-Moment: Slowing down the action so that each panel represents only a split second of time.

Smear: An art technique used to show fast motion where a character or object appears stretched across the panel.

Framing & "Camera" Angles
Establishing Shot: Usually the first panel of a scene, showing the environment so the reader knows where they are.

Bird’s Eye vs. Worm’s Eye: High-angle (looking down) versus low-angle (looking up) shots.

Extreme Close-Up (ECU): Focusing on a very specific detail, like a finger pulling a trigger or a sweating brow.

Dutch Angle: Tilting the horizon line to create a sense of unease or disorientation.
